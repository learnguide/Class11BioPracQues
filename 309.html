6.Why are nerve impulses conducted more rapidly in myelinated nerve fiber than in a
non – myelinated one? Explain.
Ans.
In a myelinated nerve fiber, the lipid rich myelin acts as an insulator and
depolarization occurs in the nodes of Ranvier where myelin sheath is absent. Since the
action potential jumps from one node of Ravines to another, the conduction becomes faster
and such a type of conduction is called saltatory conduction.
In a non–myelinated fiber, the depolarization occurs all along its length and hence
conduction becomes slower.
