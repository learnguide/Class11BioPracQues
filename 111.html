2.What is wood? What are its different types?
Ans.
Botanically, a secondary xylem is called as wood. It is formed by the metabolism of the
plant i.e. secondary growth by cambium & constitutes the bulk of plant body in dicot stem &
dicot root. Wood can be classified into following categories.
(i)
Hardwood:- It is wood produced by angiosperms. It consists mainly of xylem vessels &
hence called porous wood.
(ii)
Soft wood:- It is wood produced by gymnosperm. It consists mainly of xylem trachieds &
hence called non-porous wood.
(iii)
Heart wood:_ It is the central core of wood formed during secondary growth. It consists
of dead cells. The cells are dark in color due to the presence of extractives like gums, resins,
tannins, etc.
(iv)
Sap wood:_ It is the peripneral part of wood formed during secondary growth. It consists
of living cells. The cells are lighter in colour as extractives are absent.
(v)
Early wood:- It is the wood formed during favorable season. Vessels & tracheids formed
are larger in dimensions.
(vi)
Late wood:- It is the wood formed during unfavorable seasons. The vessels & tracheids
formed are smaller in dimensions.
