CBSE Class 12 Biology
Important Questions
Chapter 16
Digestion and Absorption
3 Marks Questions
1.How is DNA content in our food digested in the body?
Ans.
DNA content is digested in the intestinal part of our alimentary canal by the enzymes
present in pancreatic juice & sucous entericus.
2.How would it affect the digestion of proteins if there is blockade in the pancreatic
duct?
Ans.
Pancreatic duct in addition to pancreatic juice brings bile juice also. The pancreatic
juice contains many enzymes which are as fallows-
a)
Trypsin – It acts on protein, proteases and peptones and converts them into amino acids.
b)
Amylopsin – It acts on starch and converts it into soluble sugars.
c)
steapsin or lipase – It emulsify the fats and convert them into fatty acids and glycerol.
Hence, if there is a blockade in the pancreatic duct then there will be no digestion of
proteins, fats and starch because the digestive enzymes will be absent.
