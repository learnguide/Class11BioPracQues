Digestion and Absorption
2 Marks Questions
1.What is the role of micelles in the fat absorption?
Ans.
During digestion, the fat in the intestine is converted to monoglycerides diglycerides
and fatty acids which are insoluble in water. They cannot be directly absorbed from the
intestinal contents. They are first incorporated into small, spherical and water soluble
droplets called micelles by bile salts. It is from these micelles that fatty acids, glycosides,
sterols and fat soluble vitamins are absorbed into the intestinal cells.
2.Give two functions of trypsin?
Ans. 1)
Trypsin converts chymotrypsinogen into chymotrypsin.
2)
Trypsin acts on proteoses and peptones and convert them into peptides.
Trypsin + Peptones + Proteoses
→
Peptides.
3.What are the specific functions of food?
Ans.
Specific functions of food are –
(i)
Food on oxidation inside the body supplies energy to perform various functions.
(ii)
It serves to supply the material for growth and development of the body.
(iii)
It also serves as a reserve material mainly as fat and glycogen. These can be utilized at
the time of emergency.
