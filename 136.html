1.Describe the ultrastructure of a cillium or flagellum?
Ans.
Cilia & flagella have fundamentally the same structures. Each cilium or flagellum
consists of eleven microtubules. These microtubules are arranged in two radii. Of these, nine
are doublets. These are situated at the periphery & the remaining two are single
microtubules situated in the centre. The microtubules are enclosed in a cytoplasmic matrix
to form an axial filament. The outer tubules are 360 A

From the centre arise nine secondary filaments. These are connected with tubules of the
outer doublets.
