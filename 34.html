18.What is heterospory? What is its significance?
Ans.
Heterospory refers to the production of two kinds of spores in pteridophytes eg.
salivinia and selaginella produces two kinds of spores macrospores & microspores. These
mega & microspores germinate & then give rise to male & female gametophytes. The female
gametophyte is retained on parent sporophyte for variable period of time. So, this event is a
precursor to seed habit.
19.What are gymnosperms? What are its four classes?
Ans.
Gymnosperms are vascular plants with naked seeds. The seeds are exposed on surface
of sporophyll. The reproductive organs are usually borne in cones on which spores are
spirally arranged. Gymnosperms are classified into four groups Conifers, Cycads, Ginkgo,
Gnetophytes.
