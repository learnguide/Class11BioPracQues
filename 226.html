2.What does the sigmoid growth curve of a population mean?
Ans.
In biological organization growth occurs at many levels, from the molecular level upto
the ecosystem level. It can be measured at different levels such as the growth of cell
organism or population. If it is measured in length, area, volume, mass or number of cells or
individuals and plotted against time, and s-shaped curve is obtained. This is known as
sigmoid curve. An analysis of this curve shows a lag phase during which slow growth occurs.
This gradually attains a rapid growth, followed by a period of slow growth and ultimately a
decline called stationary phase. Since the same patterns of growth is sigmoid curve observed
at all levels of organization it is said to be universal.
