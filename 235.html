5.How is food absorbed?
Ans.
The food eaten up by individuals is in complex form. The digestive glands secrete
enzymes in different parts of alimentary canal and digest it into simpler form, mainly
soluble form. The digested food consist of fatty acid and glycerol are absorbed through
intestinal wall through lacteals. The sugars, amino acids, salts and water passed into blood
circulation, water absorption takes place in colon (large intestine).

7.If a major part of the small intestine of a mammal be removed, will this affect
absorption of food?
Ans.
The major part of the food is absorbed only in the small intestine, only some part of
water is absorbed in the stomach. So, if the major part of the small intestine is removed it
would seriously affect the absorption of digested food.
